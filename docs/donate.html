No VC. No corporate backing. No data sales. One engineer, one home GPU, and donations.

What this funds

  • Phase 4 — base-model scaling. The 1.8M-param Eli you just talked to proves the architecture. Scaling to ~50M params with a BPE tokenizer is what makes Eli fluent. RTX 4060 will do it in ~2 weeks of overnight runs. Donations buy electricity + occasional rented GPU hours for stretched runs.
  • Floor on the engineer's tooling. Claude Code's $200/month is the development floor. Hitting that recurring donation level lets the project pay for itself, end of story.
  • Phase 6 multimodal. Vision + voice teacher models run on Groq free tier, but training the small runtime models is what takes hours of compute that have to come from somewhere.

Phases are listed publicly in docs/roadmap_to_perfect_interface.md with effort estimates and decision gates. Every donation level has a phase it unblocks; the math is in the open.

What this doesn't fund

Listed explicitly to be the kind of project that posts these things explicitly:

  • No paid hosting tiers (the demo stays free even at high traffic — GitHub Pages + Cloudflare absorbs it).
  • No social-media spend. The author can't peoplee. There is no team running Twitter / Discord / TikTok to fund.
  • No staffing. One engineer + autonomous-after-deploy AI agents. Donations stay 1:1 with compute + the author's tooling floor.
  • No equity to sell. There is none. This is open-source MIT.
